How to deliver great customer service with haply

Edited January 6th 2023

Jack Andersen IT consultant

Hiring the wrong person can cause many problems. Finding a replacement is expensive, and rapid staff turnover is rarely good for team morale. 15 px

When you have to hire a customer success manager, it can be particularly problematic if you choose the wrong one. This position is very important in terms of creating and nurturing long-term customer relationships. If you choose someone who is a poor match, you will likely experience lower retention rates. And in the long run, this means lower turnover.

A customer success manager’s role may immediately resemble that of a customer service manager, but they are two different types of jobs.

Customer service managers are reactive. They solve problems as quickly as possible when they arise. They usually have a short-term relationship with the customers.

Customer success managers are proactive. They build long-lasting relationships with customers. They try to understand their goals and possible business challenges. With that knowledge, they can predict potential problems the customer may face and do something to avoid them. To help find a strong candidate for the position of customer success manager, we have selected 30 important interview questions that you can ask during the job interview.

You will also learn what to look out for in the applicants’ answers. That way you can assess whether they are a good match for the position and for your organisation.
